Lesson 7: Scam Prevention Quick Guide

Use this short guide when you want to review the most common scam warning signs before connecting a wallet, sending funds, or trusting a message.

Warning Signs to Slow Down For

  • Fake websites that look almost correct but use the wrong domain.
  • Messages asking for your seed phrase, password, remote access, or urgent payment.
  • Pressure, countdowns, or guaranteed-return promises designed to rush you.

Address Poisoning Reminder

  • Do not trust recent transaction history alone when copying a wallet address.
  • Lookalike addresses can be placed in your history to trick you into sending funds to the wrong place.
  • Check the full address and use saved trusted addresses whenever possible.

Safer Habits

  • Use bookmarked official sites for important exchanges, wallets, and services.
  • Send a small test amount first when the address or network is new.
  • Pause and verify before every important click, copy, or approval.
Quick reminder: in crypto, slowing down is often the safest security habit.
